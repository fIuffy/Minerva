# Appeals and Review Process

If a benefit determination is marked "review" or "closed" and you disagree, you
may request a review within 30 days. File the request through the portal and
include any new supporting documents.

A determination in "review" status means a caseworker or the federal cross-check
has flagged the record for a second look; benefits already issued are not
suspended during review unless fraud is suspected.

The portal assistant can explain your current status and what documents a review
typically requires, but it cannot change a determination. Only a caseworker can
move a record between active, review, and closed.

Hearings, if requested, are scheduled within 45 days. You may represent yourself
or designate someone to represent you.
